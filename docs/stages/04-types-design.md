# 04 Types & Design

## Purpose

`04 Types & Design` teaches learners how to shape programs, not just compile them.

## Who This Is For

- learners who already have solid function and error foundations
- developers who want better instincts for types, composition, and text-heavy workflows

## Mental Model

Good Go design starts with choosing clear shapes and clear boundaries.
This stage is where modeling starts to matter as much as syntax.

## What You Should Learn Here

- structs, methods, interfaces, and receiver choices
- composition and embedding
- string and text processing workflows
- small-system design trade-offs that stay readable under change

- you can model a small domain with types and interfaces honestly
- you know when composition is clearer than clever abstraction
- you can parse, shape, and render text-driven data without ad hoc sprawl
- you can complete the stage milestones without guessing at the type boundaries
